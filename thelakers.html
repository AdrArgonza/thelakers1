About Them

  • The Los Angeles Lakers are an American professional basketball team based in Los Angeles. The Lakers compete in the National Basketball Association, as a member of the league's Western Conference in the Pacific Division.

  • The Lakers are one of the most successful and popular professional franchises in all American sports. The franchise has won a combined 16 Basketball Association of America (BAA) and National Basketball Association (NBA) titles, second in league history to their rival the Boston Celtics.

    • NBA Championships

  • The Lakers won the 2010, 2009, 2002, 2001, 2000, 1988, 1987, 1985, 1982, 1980, 1972, 1954, 1953, 1952, 1950, and 1949 NBA Championships.

    • Fun Facts

  • The LA Lakers started as the Minneapolis Lakers. The team name references Minnesota's nickname, "Land of 10,000 Lakes."

  • Kobe Bryant-one of the team's most legendary players was named after Kobe beef, after his parents saw it on a restaurant menu.

  • 19-year-old rookie Brandon Ingram is the youngest player on the team, with a whopping 7'3" wingspan.

  • At 36-years-old, former player Luke Walton is now the youngest head coach in the NBA. He was recently described by forward Thomas Robinson as "A cool-ass dude."
